172 - Are we in a Mass AI Psychosis

Podcast · Money & Business · 13 Mar 2026 · 12m · source

⚡ BOTTOM LINE

The podcast host argues we may be experiencing "mass AI psychosis" — a collective overreaction where markets and public perception swing wildly between AI euphoria and scepticism, driven more by hype cycles and promises than verifiable technological reality.

📝 THESIS

AI companies and markets are trapped in a boom-bust psychology cycle, oscillating between unrealistic optimism when grand projects are announced and sceptical despair when actual capabilities fail to match marketing claims. This pattern resembles the initial dot-com bubble, requiring careful navigation to identify which AI applications will have real staying power versus which represent "AI vaporware."

💡 KEY INSIGHTS

  1. The Stargate Project exemplifies AI "vaporware flexing" — The $500 billion AI infrastructure project announced with fanfare appears to be facing significant funding and control issues behind the scenes, despite initial market optimism.1 [✓]

  2. Markets react more to AI announcements than actual capabilities — Stock movements for companies like Oracle and IBM have shown volatility based on AI partnership announcements rather than demonstrated technological breakthroughs.2

  3. Claude's COBOL claims triggered market panic without verification — Anthropic's announcement that Claude could modernise COBOL code caused IBM's worst daily drop since 2000 (13.2%), despite limited evidence of actual capability.3 [✓]

  4. Consulting partnerships signal AI's enterprise maturity shift — OpenAI's partnerships with McKinsey, BCG, Accenture and Capgemini represent a strategic move from technology development to enterprise implementation and workflow integration.4 [✓]

  5. The Theranos comparison highlights AI's credibility problem — Like Theranos promising impossible blood-testing technology, AI companies face scepticism about achieving AGI through current large language model approaches, raising questions about "fake it till you make it" incentives.5

  6. General public is transitioning from "wow demo" to "reality check" phase — After three years of impressive demos, users are entering a stage where they realise AI tools often "work nothing like advertised" in practical applications.

🎯 STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

For investors: Distinguish between AI announcements and demonstrable capabilities — stock reactions to partnership news may create volatility opportunities rather than reflect fundamental value changes.

For enterprises: Balance AI adoption urgency with verification requirements — consulting partnerships indicate AI is moving from experimental to implementable, but require careful vendor capability assessment.

The AI industry is entering a credibility consolidation phase where marketing claims must align with practical delivery, creating both scepticism and opportunity for those who can separate signal from noise.
