📄 7 New AI Features To Save You Time: From Excel to Google Workspace and AI Agents

⚡ BOTTOM LINE

Major tech companies are racing to embed AI directly into productivity workflows, eliminating context switching and moving beyond chatbots toward autonomous agents that can work alongside users across documents, spreadsheets, and local systems.

📝 THESIS

The AI productivity landscape is shifting from standalone chatbots to deeply integrated, context-aware assistants that work within existing applications, with particular focus on spreadsheets, autonomous agents, and unified knowledge bases—driven by competitive pressure among Google, Microsoft, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Perplexity to capture enterprise users.

💡 KEY INSIGHTS

  1. Google’s “Help me create” mode finally unifies Gemini across Workspace — After years of fragmented AI features, Google now offers a “Help me create” tool that pulls context from Gmail, Drive, and Chat to build documents, spreadsheets, and presentations directly within Docs, Sheets, and Slides, repositioning Drive as an “active knowledge base”1. [✓]5

  2. Perplexity’s “Personal Computer” aims to simplify OpenClaw-like automation — Perplexity has launched a waitlist-only product that runs on a dedicated Mac mini 24/7, merging local apps with cloud-based AI agents while providing security safeguards and audit trails, positioning itself as a more accessible alternative to OpenClaw2. [✓]6

  3. Microsoft’s Copilot Cowork integrates Anthropic’s technology for autonomous task execution — Microsoft has partnered with Anthropic to integrate Claude’s Cowork technology into Copilot, enabling multi-step workflow execution across Microsoft 365 that can run in the background with user checkpoints3. [✓]7

  4. OpenAI targets financial professionals with ChatGPT Excel add-in — OpenAI released a GPT-5.4-powered Excel add-in with direct integrations to financial data providers like FactSet, Moody’s, and S&P Global, enabling financial modeling and analysis without leaving the spreadsheet4. [✓]8

  5. AI Skills adoption signals enterprise workflow standardisation — OpenAI quietly rolled out Skills (reusable workflows that apply automatically) to most paid business plans, directly competing with Anthropic’s similar feature and addressing the pain point of repeating instructions across conversations5.

  6. Spreadsheet automation emerges as a key battleground — Three different companies (Microsoft, Google, OpenAI) shipped AI directly inside spreadsheets this week, indicating a strategic focus on this high-frequency productivity surface for finance and analysis work6.

  7. Scheduled automation becomes mainstream with Claude Code’s /schedule command — Anthropic added cron-style scheduling to Claude Code, enabling background task execution for things like overnight monitoring, bug fixing, and weekly reports, targeting the same use cases that made OpenClaw popular7. [✓]9

🎯 STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

For finance professionals: The ChatGPT Excel add-in with financial data integrations could dramatically reduce time spent on manual data gathering and modeling, though adoption will depend on data accuracy and security compliance.

For enterprise IT decision-makers: The proliferation of AI features within productivity suites creates integration complexity but also opportunity to standardise workflows and reduce shadow AI usage.

For knowledge workers: Elimination of context switching through in-app AI assistants could significantly boost productivity, but requires learning new interaction patterns and trusting automated outputs.

The convergence on spreadsheet automation suggests financial analysis and reporting remain pain points where AI can deliver immediate ROI, while the focus on scheduled tasks indicates a shift toward truly autonomous assistance rather than just on-demand help.
