## ⚡ BOTTOM LINE
Enterprises can now save millions by shifting high‑volume, low‑risk AI workloads to open‑source or cheap‑API models, but must retain closed‑source models for regulated, high‑value tasks to preserve legal protection.

---

## 📝 THESIS
The rapid convergence of open‑source model performance with frontier closed models—driven by Google’s Gemma 4 and Chinese distillation—has turned open‑source AI from a hobby into a boardroom decision point. Companies must adopt a triage‑style AI workflow, matching tasks to the most cost‑effective model while accounting for legal and security considerations.

---

## 💡 KEY INSIGHTS
1. **Gap Closure** — Open‑source models are now only 10‑30 Elo points behind top closed models, a 90% reduction from a 250‑point gap a year ago, making them competitive for many tasks.[^1]
2. **Local Capability** — Google’s Gemma 4 runs on a consumer laptop at near‑frontier performance, enabling on‑premise AI without cloud costs.[^2]
3. **Cost Savings** — DeepSeek V4 Pro pricing is $0.43 per million input tokens and $0.87 per million output tokens, roughly 25× cheaper than premium closed APIs, potentially saving enterprises $1‑8 million annually on bulk workloads.[^3][✓]
4. **Legal Trade‑offs** — Closed‑source enterprise offerings provide IP indemnification and warranty, which open‑source licenses (MIT, Apache 2.0) lack, creating hidden liability for regulated use.[^4]
5. **Agentic Shift** — 24/7 local AI agents now run continuously, allowing enterprises to automate summarisation, extraction, and classification at scale without recurring API fees.[^5]
6. **Strategic Triage** — Treat AI model selection like emergency‑room triage: reserve high‑cost, high‑risk models for critical tasks and deploy cheap open models for routine work.[^6]
7. **Future Trend** — Recursive self‑improvement will produce specialised, lightweight models for niche tasks, further expanding the open‑source ecosystem.[^7]
