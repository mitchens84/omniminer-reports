## 📝 THESIS

2026 marks the pivotal year AI transitioned from "locked away" chat windows to an industrial, factory-scale technology that will permeate all digital experiences and robotics. This transformation is enabled by massive hardware deals and partnerships, resulting in an expanding, multi-winner landscape rather than a single dominant player.[^1]

## 💡 KEY INSIGHTS

1. **AI is becoming a "factory"** — The industry has moved beyond isolated AI experiments to industrial-scale infrastructure capable of continuous, high-volume operation across training, fine-tuning, and inference. This "AI factory" model enables AI to embed in every digital surface and rapidly advance into robotics.[^1]

2. **Nvidia retains dominance but within a growing ecosystem** — The Vera Rubin platform, unveiled at GTC 2026, confirms Nvidia's position as the biggest player in AI chipsets for the foreseeable future, while the "AI factory" concept standardizes large-scale deployment.[^2]

3. **Strategic partnerships create multiple winners** — OpenAI's custom silicon work with Broadcom, and Microsoft's revised partnership granting it IP access to those designs, demonstrates how second sources and custom silicon can gain meaningful share without displacing incumbents.[^3]

4. **The AI hardware pie is expanding** — Anthropic's deal for up to one million Google TPUs (worth tens of billions) exemplifies how the overall market is growing rapidly, allowing multiple players to thrive simultaneously rather than engaging in zero-sum competition.[^4]
