## ⚡ BOTTOM LINE

The 2026 AHA/ACC dyslipidemia guidelines shift to lifetime risk assessment, adding reproductive factors for women and mandating universal Lp(a) screening.

---

## 📝 THESIS

New cardiovascular prevention guidelines emphasise treating dyslipidemia earlier and more aggressively, incorporate women-specific risk factors, and identify Lp(a) as a mandatory screening target.

---

## 💡 KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Lifetime risk paradigm** — Guidelines now combine 10-year and 30-year ASCVD risk using PREVENT-ASCVD equations, prompting earlier intervention.*[^1]*

2. **Women's reproductive factors** — Early menopause (<45), preeclampsia, gestational diabetes, gestational hypertension, and preterm delivery are formally recognised as cardiovascular risk enhancers.*[^2]*

3. **Universal Lp(a) screening** — All adults should receive at least one lipoprotein(a) test; levels ≥125 nmol/L (50 mg/dL) confer ~1.4-fold increased ASCVD risk, ≥250 nmol/L doubles it.*[^3]*

4. **Lower LDL targets** — Very high-risk patients (e.g., established ASCVD) now have LDL-C goal of <55 mg/dL, reinforcing aggressive lipid lowering.*[^1]*

5. **Youth considerations** — Pharmacotherapy is now recommended for young adults with LDL-C ≥160 mg/dL or strong family history of premature ASCVD, in addition to familial hypercholesterolemia.*[^1]*
