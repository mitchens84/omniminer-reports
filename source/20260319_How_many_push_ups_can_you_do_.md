## 🔍 FACT CHECK

> ✓ **VERIFIED** — The chair stand test (also called sit-to-stand test) is a validated functional assessment tool used in clinical settings and fitness testing for older adults. Research confirms it assesses lower extremity strength, balance, and fall risk[^7].

> ✓ **VERIFIED** — Body composition machines like DEXA (Dual-energy X-ray Absorptiometry) and BIA (Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis) are widely available in gyms and medical facilities to measure fat mass, muscle mass, and visceral fat[^8].

> ✓ **VERIFIED** — Sarcopenia (age-related muscle loss) affects approximately 30% of older adults and significantly impacts physical independence, with chair stand inability being a key indicator of functional decline[^9].

## 🎯 STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

**For younger adults:** Use push-up maximum as a baseline strength metric and track improvement over 4-8 week training cycles, combining with periodic body composition analysis for holistic progress tracking.

**For older adults:** Prioritise chair stand ability as a key functional metric—if unable to stand without using hands, this should be an immediate focus for strength training and balance work.

**For fitness professionals:** Incorporate age-appropriate functional assessments alongside traditional gym metrics, recognising that simple tests often provide the most clinically relevant health information.

Functional strength assessment provides immediate, actionable health feedback that correlates directly with independence and quality of life across the lifespan.
