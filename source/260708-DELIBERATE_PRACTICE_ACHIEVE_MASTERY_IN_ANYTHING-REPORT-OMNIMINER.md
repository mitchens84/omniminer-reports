1. **Quantified gains** — Meta‑analysis shows deliberate practice accounts for 26% of variance in games, 21% in music, 18% in sports<sup>[1]</sup>.
2. **Define & drill** — Break a skill into discrete elements (e.g., serve then footwork in tennis) and practice each deliberately.
3. **Plan, reflect, note** — Use a notebook to record what worked, what didn’t, and experiment on the next session.
4. **Slow execution** — Begin at a pace that ensures correct form; speed up only after neural pathways stabilise.
5. **Limited session length** — 15 min for younger learners, up to 60 min for adults; switch focus every ~10 min to sustain attention.
6. **Micro‑tracking** — Measure milliseconds, milligrams, or repetitions; visible tiny gains boost motivation.
7. **Coach modelling** — John Wooden’s sock‑shoe drill exemplifies how teaching fundamentals maximises practice efficiency<sup>[2]</sup>.
