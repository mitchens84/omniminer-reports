## 📝 THESIS

The speaker argues that we are collectively climbing a new "skill tree" in the AI era where the primary competence is not raw coding speed but the ability to deliberately design, condition, and orchestrate AI workflows while maintaining human authority. Organizations that recognise and scale these skills will achieve 10x speedups; those clinging to traditional hierarchies will fall behind.

## 💡 KEY INSIGHTS

1. **The perception of falling behind is accurate** — The "stack is different" now; we're all learning a new operational paradigm for knowledge work[^1]. The feeling reflects a real structural shift, not personal failure.

2. **The new core skills are orchestration, not just tool use** — Success requires: separating generation from decision-making; conditioning AI systems through artifacts and constraints; preserving human authority; building workflows rather than isolated prompts; and creating compound systems with evals and feedback loops[^1]. These are the deliberate practices of the new skill tree.

3. **Technical is now for everyone** — The emerging definition of "technical" means mastering uncertainty and maintaining authority while orchestrating AI, not just coding ability[^1]. This democratises the highest-value work.

4. **Organisational winners will be those who scale the new skill tree** — The 10x speedups will come from organisations that understand and systematically teach these skills across their workforce, not from individual tool proficiency[^1].
