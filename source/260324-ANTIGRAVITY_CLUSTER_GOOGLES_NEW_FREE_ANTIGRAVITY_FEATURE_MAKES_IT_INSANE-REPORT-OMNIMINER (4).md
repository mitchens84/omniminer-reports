## ⚡ BOTTOM LINE

Treat Google Antigravity as a cluster of specialized agents, models, and modes rather than a single monolithic chatbot to achieve higher quality output and more sustainable quota usage.

---

## 🎯 OBJECTIVE

Provide a systematic method (“AntiGravity Cluster”) for orchestrating Antigravity’s features—task splitting, model/mode routing, persistent instructions, parallelism, and continuous feedback—to maximise performance and efficiency.

---

## 📋 PREREQUISITES

- Access to Google Antigravity IDE (free or paid)
- Understanding of your project structure
- Ability to select models and switch between Planning and Fast modes
- Workspace Rules/Workflows feature (available in most builds)

---

## ⚙️ PROCESS

### Phase 1: Plan & Split Tasks
1. **Start in Planning Mode**. Ask Antigravity to inspect your repository and create an implementation plan[^1].  
2. **Split work into independent clusters**: architecture, backend, frontend, testing, verification.  
3. **Number tasks within each cluster** (e.g., B1, B2; F1, F2) to keep them atomic and routable[^1].

### Phase 2: Route Models & Modes
4. **Select one cluster to execute**. Switch to Fast Mode for small tasks; stay in Planning Mode for complex blocks[^1].  
5. **Choose model based on task complexity**: use faster models (Gemini 3 Flash) for simple edits, stronger reasoning models (Gemini 3 Pro) for architecture, debugging, and planning[^1].  

### Phase 3: Persistent Guidance & Context Hygiene
6. **Define Workspace Rules/Workflows** to encode recurring standards (code style, review process, test generation). Reuse them instead of rewriting expectations[^1].  
7. **Keep conversations focused** on one lane of work. If a thread becomes bloated, start a fresh conversation with a concise handoff summary[^1].  
8. **Anchor the agent early** with your tech stack, main entry points, important folders, and files to avoid[^1].

### Phase 4: Parallelism & Steering
9. **Run independent clusters in separate conversations or agents** to achieve parallelism[^1].  
10. **Continuously steer using artifacts** (plans, diffs, walkthroughs) via comments rather than issuing large correction prompts after the fact[^1].

### Phase 5: Quota Awareness
11. **Remember that usage is tied to the amount of work performed, not just request count**[^✓]. Batch trivial edits, use Fast Mode for low‑risk changes, and avoid rescanning the entire repository when only a subset is relevant[^1].
