## 📝 THESIS

A medical content creator summarises three key updates from the 2026 AHA/ACC dyslipidemia guidelines: extended risk assessment windows, gender-specific risk factors, and mandatory Lp(a) screening, all pointing to a more proactive approach to cardiovascular prevention.

---

## 💡 KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Extended risk assessment horizon** — Guidelines now evaluate both 10-year and 30-year cardiovascular risk, shifting treatment earlier in the disease trajectory with more aggressive LDL targets for higher-risk patients.[^1]

2. **Recognition of female-specific risk factors** — Early menopause (before age 45), preeclampsia, and gestational diabetes are now formally acknowledged as independent cardiovascular risk factors, addressing historical gender gaps in risk stratification.[^1]

3. **Universal Lp(a) screening** — One-time lifetime testing for lipoprotein(a) is now recommended for all adults, as elevated Lp(a) represents a genetically determined, independent risk factor for atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease.[^1]

### Concepts & Frameworks
- **Lp(a) (Lipoprotein(a))** — Genetic, LDL-like particle that increases clotting and atherosclerosis risk; levels remain stable throughout life
- **Dyslipidemia** — Abnormal blood lipid levels, including elevated LDL-C, triglycerides, or low HDL-C
